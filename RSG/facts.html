Geography

Russia is a transcontinental country, extending halfway around the northern hemisphere and covering much of eastern and northeastern Europe and the whole of northern Asia.

The Urals are the oldest mountains in the world, and Lake Baikal is the world’s deepest lake and the largest reservoir of freshwater on earth.

Russia covers one-seventh of the total land of our planet and neighbors more countries than any other country on earth.

Ladoga Lake is the largest one in Europe. Its area makes 18,400 sq. km., average depth is 51 m, and the largest one is up to 230 m.

Moscow Population

Moscow is the biggest city in Europe. Population is 10.5 million. Statistics say that Moscow’s population is expected to be sixteen million by 2020.

Moscow Population

Moscow actually has more billionaires living in the area than any other city in the world. With a total of seventy-four billionaires, Moscow beats New York City’s statistic by three.

Lake Baikal area is 31.5 thousand sq. km. The Baikal is the deepest lake in Russia; it is a large world reservoir for freshwater (23,000 km³).

Russia is the only state in the world on the territory of which there are twelve seas.

The closest point between Russia and America is only 4 km long.

The way from Moscow to Chicago is closer than from Chicago to Rio de Janeiro.

Russia has borders with fifteen countries: Azerbaijan, Belarus, China, Estonia, Finland, Georgia, Kazakhstan, North Korea, Latvia, Lithuania, Mongolia, Norway, Poland, and Ukraine.

Russia is also home to the world’s largest active volcano Klyuchevskaya Sopka, which has a height of 4,750 m (15,584 ft.).

Russia is known as the lungs of Europe since it has the largest forest reserves in the world, and the amount of carbon dioxide absorbed by it is second only to the rainforests in the Amazon.

Russian Volga is the longest river of Europe. The Volga River rises northwest of Moscow and extends southward some 3,700 km through the whole central part of the country.

In January 16, 1820, Russian expedition led by Thaddeus Bellingshausen and Mikhail Lazarev discovered Antarctica.

For Russians, Ural is a jewelry box with precious stones because more than two hundred minerals and precious stones are in the depths of these mountains (asbestos, rock crystals, malachite, beryl, jasper, zircon, gold).

The Trans-Siberian from Vladivostok to Moscow will take you 152 hours and 27 minutes if you do it nonstop.

In the Russian city Oimyakon, the lowest temperature of air registered in year 1933 was −67.7 °C.

There are seven towns in the Golden Ring of Russia.

During the last days of June, a phenomenon known as the White Nights can be seen in parts of Russia. It is a time when night remains bright as the sun does not sink below the horizon.

Population

In terms of population, Russia is the ninth largest country in the world, even though it’s the world’s biggest country.

Russia is multinational and multicultural. Russia has got more than one hundred nationalities—Russians, Ukrainians, Tatars, Jews, Bashkirs—it would take a separate page to list them all.

There are approximately ten million more women than men in Russia.

Culture

There are 221 museums, 2,000 libraries, more than 80 theaters, 100 concert organizations, 45 galleries, 62 cinemas, and 80 club establishments of culture in Saint Petersburg.

The Russian State Library is the biggest in Europe and second in the world after the Library of Congress in the USA. The Russian State Library is located in Moscow and was founded in 1862.

The Metro of Saint Petersburg is the world’s deepest subway (about 100 m deep).

Russians celebrate New Year two times a year (first of January and Orthodox style New Year, fourteenth of January).

Where is the largest McDonald’s in the world? In Moscow and in Beijing (seven hundred seats each).

The intelligence level of Russian people is considered up to 99 percent. There are more than six hundred universities in the country.

Saint Petersburg

Saint Petersburg is the most northern city with a population of more than one million people in the world.

Russia gave birth to many great artists, scientists, and writers: Dostoevsky, Chekhov, Tolstoy, Pushkin, Aivazovsky and Vasnetsov, Gogol and Turgenev, and Lomonosov and Karamzin.

The UNESCO World Heritage List includes twenty-four Russian heritage sites, natural and cultural properties, power points, and famous historical sites.

Russia has some of the best art institutions in the world, like Bolshoi Theater and Pushkin Art Gallery in Moscow and Mariinsky Theater and the Hermitage in Saint Petersburg.

Twenty-seven Russians have been Nobel Prize laureates. The list includes the following great names: Bunin, Pasternak, Gorbachev, Landau, Sakharov, Solgenicin, Kapitsa, and many others.

Saint Petersburg

Saint Petersburg territory is covered by water by 10 percent.

Ushanka (ear-flaps hat) is a traditional Russian winter hat with ear flaps that can be tied up at the chin to protect the ears from cold.

Red stars worked for a long time as a Red Army symbol. After the end of the Red Army, red stars can be seen only on the top of Kremlin towers and on the bottom of the Baltic Sea.

Valenki is traditional Russian winter footwear. Their name itself means “made by felting.” Genuine valenki are made of wool felt.

The wooden spoon is made of wood and thoroughly painted. This spoon is more than just a spoon; it is one of the most portable musical instruments.

Samovar is the ancient version of the electric kettle. Without electricity a samovar worked on coal and produced boiling water.

Lapti are typical Russian shoes, invented in wild, wild Russia long, long time ago. Lapti are shoes made of bast and laces which were extremely popular among Russian peasants.

The Russian Orthodox church still uses the old Julian calendar; therefore, its Christmas celebration falls on January 7, thirteen days after the West. It’s a day of both solemn ritual and joyous celebration.

Vodka was introduced in Russia in the period from 1448 to 1474. Less than one hundred years later, the first czar tavern was set up in Moscow.

Russia has forty UNESCO Biosphere Reserves and twenty-three World Heritage Sites.

Carl Fabergé, the famous goldsmith, designed a number of Easter Eggs for the Russian royal family.

Habits

Russians never shake hands over a doorway; they believe it leads to arguments.

Many Russian people prefer to spend their holidays and free time in the summer houses outside the cities which are called dacha.

Hospitality plays a significant role in the Russian mentality and character; once you make friends with Russian people, you will surely value this unique feature of the so-called wide Russian soul.

In Russia, men never give an even amount of flowers to a lady; it is a bad luck and is associated with funerals.

Russians usually wear a wedding ring on the right hand.

When you have a visitor (friend or anyone else), you have to offer them at least a coffee or tea with cookies; it’s rude if you don’t feed your guest.

When a Russian couple gets married, they usually have a big reception ceremony where everyone yells “Gor’ko,” which literally means “bitter,” and then the groom has to kiss the bride.

Russians are quite friendly and open. Friends and relatives always come to visit, usually without a phone call. It’s normal if a friend of yours or a relative comes over without asking.

Language

Russian language does not include a word for the or a, which is good because these often lead to confusion in other languages.

Russian language uses the Cyrillic alphabet instead of the Latin and is one of the five most spoken languages in the world.

Railroads

The Trans-Siberian Railway (connecting Moscow and Vladivostok) is the longest railway in the world.

Science

Soviet Cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin was the first human to journey into outer space when his Vostok spacecraft completed an orbit of the Earth on April 12, 1961.

The first artificial satellite of Earth was Russian. Sputnik (pronounced Spoot-nik, not Sput-nik) was launched on October 4, 1957—the beginning of the Space Age.

The father of modern American television was Russian electronic engineer and inventor Vladimir Zvorykin, who emigrated to the United States in 1919.

Russian chemist Dmitri Mendeleev created the periodic table of elements by arranging them according to scientific laws. The whole world uses this periodic table.

The most famous computer game, Tetris, was created by Russian programmer Alexey Pajitnov in 1985. The game, first available in the Soviet Union, appeared in the West in 1986.

Russia was the first to send a dog, Laika, into space.

History

Russia adopted equal rights before the USA. In 1918, Russian women were granted suffrage. The US has only granted the voting rights to women in 1920.

In the eighteenth century, Russia was the third largest empire in earth’s history; it covered the area from Poland in Europe to Alaska in North America.

Rasputin was an unscrupulous mystic who was able to check the hemophilia of the heir to the throne. He gained influence over the Czarina and her husband, the Czar Nicholas II.

The Russian royal family was executed in Ekaterinburg in 1918.
